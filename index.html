Poker Rules & Strategy

Position (Who acts when)

In poker, the order of action rotates clockwise every hand. The later you act, the more information you have about what your opponents will do.

  • UTG (Under the Gun): Acts first. Has no information. Must play very strong hands (e.g. AA, KK, AKs).
  • HJ (Hijack) & CO (Cutoff): Middle/Late positions. Can play slightly weaker hands.
  • BTN (Button): Acts last post-flop. Most powerful position. Can play a very wide range of hands to steal the blinds.
  • SB (Small Blind) & BB (Big Blind): Forced to put money in before the cards are dealt, and have to act first post-flop.

Preflop Strategy

When the action folds to you, you are deciding whether to Fold or Raise First In (RFI). “Limping” (just calling the big blind) is generally a bad strategy for beginners. You should either raise to take control, or fold.

Advanced Strategy Concepts (via PokerVIP)

  • UTG Tightness: Being Under the Gun puts you at a severe informational disadvantage. You must play very a tight range because you face actions from the entire table behind you.
  • Dealing with 3-Bets: A "3-Bet" is a re-raise preflop. If you open-raise from UTG and face a 3-bet, your opponent usually has a very strong hand. You must defend cautiously or 4-bet with premiums.
  • Implied Odds: The money you expect to win after making a big hand (like hitting a Set with a pocket pair). Small pairs can sometimes call 3-bets if the opponent's stack is deep enough to pay you off when you hit.

Basic Hand Rankings (Strongest to Weakest)

  • Royal Flush: A♥ K♥ Q♥ J♥ T♥ (all same suit)
  • Straight Flush: 9♠ 8♠ 7♠ 6♠ 5♠ (in sequence, same suit)
  • Four of a Kind: Q♠ Q♥ Q♦ Q♣ 4♠
  • Full House: K♠ K♥ K♣ 2♦ 2♠ (three of a kind + pair)
  • Flush: Any five cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: 5 6 7 8 9 (in sequence, mixed suits)
  • Three of a Kind: 7 7 7 A 2
  • Two Pair: J J 9 9 4
  • One Pair: A A 8 5 3
  • High Card: A K 9 5 2 (Highest card plays)
